Contains all functional offerings of this library\+: convert dates between Hebrew, Gregorian and Julian calendars as well as compute certain specific times for the new moon, types of Jewish year.

\label{hconverter_8h_hebmonth}%
\hypertarget{hconverter_8h_hebmonth}{}%
\subsubsection*{Note about Hebrew month ordering. }

Although each Hebrew year starts with the month of Tishrei, Tishrei is not the first in the order of months. There are a couple of reasons for this. Nisan is called the \char`\"{}first month\char`\"{} in the Torah for most purposes. Also, on a Hebrew leap year the 13th month (adar 2) is added before Nisan, not before Tishrei. This makes it more convenient to senumerate months not in chronological order but rather starting from Nisan to the end of year (Elul), and then returning to Tishrei through Adar. Thus the Hebrew months here are represented by integers as follows\+:

\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\ttfamily Tishrei} 7 \item {\ttfamily Cheshvan} 8 \item {\ttfamily Kislev} 9 \item {\ttfamily Teveth} 10 \item {\ttfamily Shvat} 11 \item {\ttfamily Adar} 12 (also Adar I on leap years) \item {\ttfamily Adar} I\+I 13 (only on Hebrew leap years) \item {\ttfamily Nisan} 1 \item {\ttfamily Iyar} 2 \item {\ttfamily Sivan} 3 \item {\ttfamily Tamuz} 4 \item {\ttfamily Menachem} Av 5 \item {\ttfamily Elul} 6\end{DoxyItemize}
so despite a lower month index, Nisan follows {\bfseries after} Adar. For example, the date 5776-\/01-\/01 follows 5776-\/13-\/29 in yyyy-\/mm-\/dd notation.

\label{hconverter_8h_keviut}%
\hypertarget{hconverter_8h_keviut}{}%
\subsubsection*{Note about keviut }

Keviut determines layout of the Jewish year, including all holidays and readings of the Torah. It is customary to abbreviate it to 4 parameters\+:

\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries 1} day of week of Rosh Hashana, i.\+e. the \char`\"{}new year\char`\"{}, Tishrei 1 \item {\bfseries 2} day of week of first day of Pesach, i.\+e. Nisan 15 \item {\bfseries 3} combined length of Cheshvan and Kislev (see \#hc\+\_\+heb\+\_\+year\+\_\+type) \item {\bfseries 4} whether or not the year is leap\end{DoxyItemize}
Only 14 combinations of these are actually possible.
